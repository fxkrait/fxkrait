🪜 Guard Rails: ~Avoid Extremes. Protect Humanity. The Fighter > Critic. Win/Win > Zero/Sum. Traverse beyond local maximas. Let the past rest. Focus on just a few. Be better than your yesterday. Confront avoidance~ 🤻

A [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triratna][triratna]] of Douglas Adams quotes I revisit from time to time:
- ~We don't have to save the world, the world is big enough to look after itself. What we have to be concerned about, is whether or not the world we live in, will be capable of sustaining us in it. - Douglas Adams~
- ~I think it's wonderful we just sort of keep on inventing it (the simple water faucet - tweaking it, redesigning it, changing its mechanism) even though it works perfectly well, because it's the way of getting ourselves off local maximums isn't it? - Douglas Adams~
- ~Writing this book, I mean being aware of the fact that there are these dreadful apalling problems - I didn't want to set out to beat people around the head with that. Because if you beat people around the head, they'll put their head somewhere else. So the idea was to engage people's enthusiasm by entertaining them, making them laugh, and leaving them in some kind of understanding as I came across it. And creep up on them with the serious stuff about this. - Douglas Adams~


Supplemented with these dual quotes from Edward Snowden, to contextualize, frame, and bounding-box the [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polis][polis]]:
- ~I think there is an authoritarian center of gravity, that is pulling all manner of governments toward it. The Chinese government is very authoritarian. Many governments around the world are very authoritarian. But what's interesting, is that we're seeing more-liberal governments becoming more authoritarian. Covid was really a bright red warning flag, that it doesn't take much - a sense of panic, a sense of emergency - we're seeing the same thing with Israel and Hamas right now. Where we go ohp, somebodies crossed the lines, there's a 9/11 moment, and the old rules no longer apply. Classic rights will no longer be protected, the world has changed, so we as institutions will change. There's no time logistically for a vote, there's not time for a conversation, this is a emergency, don't you understand? And so we need to use the maximum extent of our capability and our technology to confront the threat... (and then the alternative to this downward and ever-restricting spiral becomes) if (liberty/freedom is) not possible under our sets of laws, can we create a new jursidiction? This is the old Declaration of Independence of cyberspace. - Edward Snowden~
- ~Even the worst of us understand that government can have a positive role - even for the most radical of us - if they are kept within the box. When government starts climbing out of that box, that's when things get dangerous. So we need to have a pretty strong box, we need to have pretty strong borders. And the only technical solutions that we have found to legal or policy problems, in the computing era, have been ones that are hard lines. Like encryption, or like the fact that this is a protocol, and you can't just go to someone and say 'take this down'. You can go to a node owner, or a relay owner (TOR), and ask nicely. But you can't just say 'do this or you go to jail', and poof it's gone... But whether it's comunications, whether it's payments, I've talked before about this idea, we need a first post-national bank. We don't need the Bank of America, we don't need the Bank of Russia, we don't need the Bank of China. We need something that is post-national, not just extra-national, post-national. Imagine a world where states are not the top-tier in deciding who gets paid, and who doesn't get paid. We need the same thing for speech. And I think protocol based communication is, like the internet, everything that we're doing now - it's TCP/IP basically. And so even if we get it wrong, even if there are dark points, we can come out of that, and things can get better as long as we keep trying. - Edward Snowden~

Then finally in completion, we have [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexamenos_graffito][Alexamenos]] reminding us to never give ourselves over completely to any local maximas, and always have our eyes towards the global maximas.
- ~The greatest trick the Devil ever pulled was convincing the world he didn’t exist.~

Lastly, Elon reminding us that actions matter. The world does not automatically get better, and evil will subsume, by definiton and by constraint, if the good is not actively propogated and maintained.
- ~I care about the reality of goodness, not the perception of it. And what I see all over the place is people who care about looking good, while doing evil. - Elon Musk~
