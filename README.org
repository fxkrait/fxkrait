Q: Will you ever become an [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Four_stages_of_awakening][arahant]]?

A: I worry that I've wasted and spent too much of my life on the carnal matters, and have lived too much of it in a undisciplined and unrestrained manner, to the point where I cannot attain such things at this point, given who I am and what I've become, my patterns and conditionings, and the time I have left. But worries are often just the source for additional worries, so we'll put that to the side for a bit, and see what I can make happen here. The least I can do is try, and give things a noble effort. What other choice do I have? Give up? That's not a choice anyone would sanely make given the situation I'm in. If Frank Yang got things really going by his 30's, I'll see what I can do. I may need to tweak the way I'm living, which is the part of the process that I'm not too fond of, to put lightly. Who genuinely likes change? Not many, including me. I guess we likely have that in common. But even our apparent friendship is impermanent. And why be friends with our limitations? Why not be friends, and on speaking terms with the truth? Everyone wants to be an arahant, but no one wants to train. I'll be the motherfucker that trains. You can call this a Rocky story if you'd like.
