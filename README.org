*The Genius of Being, Peter Ralston quotes:*

#+begin_quote
"There are many possibilities that can arise when letting go of some assumed form of identity. For example, given that you didn’t identify with being a psychologically vulnerable internal person, in all likelihood your feelings couldn’t be hurt, judgment of others would not occur, and pettiness would be a thing of the past. If you no longer identified with your self-agenda, you wouldn’t feel the drive to have your historically based needs met, nor would you be pressed to avoid anything. If you don’t hold yourself as an object, you can’t be the hidden one inside, existing as a “thing” that can be flawed or vulnerable or special."
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"What if instead, you don’t hold the feeling as bad and don’t resist it? If you let it be just that, a feeling—and perhaps embrace it for what it is—your experience would be radically different, and you wouldn’t get trapped in all the reactivity that arises from your original way of holding it."

This applies to everything you encounter—objects, others, feelings, actions, interactions, memories, ideas, or whatever. The more “commentary” you add to what is there, the more you relate to it with activities other than just perceiving or interpreting its existence. Everything you “say” about it will give it meaning to which you’ll react. None of that is what’s just there."
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"If someone says something to you that you conclude means you’re disapproved of and are bad and wrong, you will suffer a great deal and actively resist this comment, internally or interactively. But if you hear what they said as simply what they said, end of story, it stops there and you won’t go on to conclude that you are bad and wrong. In so many ways and on so many levels, this same principle applies."
#+end_quote
