#+begin_quote
"One minute there’s love, the next hate. That’s how things are. In this sense they are permanent. That’s why I say that whenever love arises, just tell it, “So!” It saves a lot of time. You don’t have to say, “Anicca, dukkha, anattā.” If you don’t want a long meditation theme, just take this simple word. If love arises, before you get really lost in it, just tell yourself, “So!” This is enough."
#+end_quote

*The Genius of Being, Peter Ralston quotes:*

#+begin_quote
"The fundamental aspect of self is simply that there is a you and other-than-you."

"Not-you is always present, whether you’re identified with a body or not. Think about it. In every moment not-you is always close at hand. Wherever you experience the sensation or awareness of self, not-self is right there with it. In this way—except for an absence of other “entities”—you can’t really be alone, ever."

"Actually, you can’t because experiencing self without an experience of other-than-self is no more possible than drawing only the inside of a circle. Self and not-self define each other. Even if there is only you in empty space, you must hold that the empty space is not-you. If there is only you, then there can be no other or not-you, so you can’t be anything at all—not even “everything,” because then you would no longer be “nothing.” Can you grasp that?"
#+end_quote
