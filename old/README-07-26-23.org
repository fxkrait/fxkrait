*The Genius of Being, Peter Ralston quotes:*

#+begin_quote
"The fundamental aspect of self is simply that there is a you and other-than-you."

"Not-you is always present, whether you’re identified with a body or not. Think about it. In every moment not-you is always close at hand. Wherever you experience the sensation or awareness of self, not-self is right there with it. In this way—except for an absence of other “entities”—you can’t really be alone, ever."

"Beyond your dependency on other and not-you for your survival, try to imagine a world in which there is nothing other than you. Actually, you can’t because experiencing self without an experience of other-than-self is no more possible than drawing only the inside of a circle. Self and not-self define each other. Even if there is only you in empty space, you must hold that the empty space is not-you. If there is only you, then there can be no other or not-you, so you can’t be anything at all—not even “everything,” because then you would no longer be “nothing.” Can you grasp that?"
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"Enlightenment supports all of these possibilities largely because it provides a consciousness of no-context. Here, there is no perception and nothing to perceive; nothing is assumed or thought; there is no space or object, no self and no experience. Here, there is no need or room for any relative distinction and so no context exists. Pure Consciousness without form or function, without location or experience, is all that remains. Since there is no “self,” there is nothing to do, protect, accomplish, or attain."
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"As long as your current self-context persists, your efforts to establish a new possibility for experience will probably be seen as a counter-movement. Since the aim is to interrupt and create an alternative to the automatic value-judgment reactivity that serves self-survival, any new possibility will probably appear as counter-intuitive. Such non-survival-oriented alternatives are found in the principles I so frequently mention, some of which are genuine honesty, being non-judgmental, experiencing everything for-itself, contemplation, and real communication."
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"No longer would anything be perceived by associating it with a life-context, so what’s perceived would be without the meaning generated by life ideals and life scenarios. What that means in practical terms is that you couldn’t be depressed, in despair, lonely, relieved, hopeful, or experience any other feeling overlay that is based on this life-referencing context."
#+end_quote

#+begin_quote
"Just as with adding any physical or mental practice, however, you must come to grips with the fact that there is a cost. You can’t change without changing. Sometimes this is just adopting new ideas; sometimes it requires altering whatever motivates certain behavior. Changing mind and perspective takes work. Unnecessary and dysfunctional assumptions and attitudes must be let go, and one is obliged to repeatedly engage new ways of experiencing self and reality through practice, contemplation, and new perspectives and experiences."
#+end_quote
